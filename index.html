About Me

I am a Postdoc researcher in the Institute for Theoretical Physics at the Heidelberg University in Germany. My work focuses on various questions related to particle collisions at the CERN-LHC and BNL-RHIC experiments. My research focuses on the description of particle collisions using Quantum Chromodynamics, jets, finite-temperature, and non-equilibrium field theories. I contribute to several ongoing measurements by providing predictions and introducing new techniques such as artificial intelligence.

Featured Publications

High-Dimensional Unfolding in Large Backgrounds

We present a ML powered code that performs unfolding in large backgrounds. We show, high dimensional unfolding significantly improves performance. Our new technique overcomes the performance limitation encoded in routins such as JER and JES.

Jul 8, 2025

No-quenching baseline for energy loss signals in oxygen-oxygen collisions

We present a next-to-leading order (NLO) baseline for energy loss signals in the upcoming oxygen-oxygen (OO) collisions at CERN's Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Our analysis includes several potential observables, such as the jet nuclear modification factor and the semi-inclusive jet-hadron nuclear modification factor. Our aim is to advance the potential for experimental detection of energy loss signals in OO collisions.

Oct 29, 2024

Isolating perturbative QCD splittings in heavy-ion collisions

This work outlines an extensive measurement plan to answer the most fundamental questions of jet quenching.

Nov 13, 2023

A unified picture of medium-induced radiation

We present a new framework in which different jet quenching models are unified. We calculate the medium modifided splitting functions in a finite plasma and resum them numerically.

Jun 6, 2022

Quarks and gluons in the Lund plane

We connect perturbative expansions with machine learning, assigning accuracy to a neural network. We provide the state of the art quark/gluon jet classifier using a graph neural network.
